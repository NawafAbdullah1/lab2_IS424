The Brown Bear

Learn more about bears

The brown bear is sometimes referred to as the bruin, from Middle English. This name originated in the fable History of Reynard the Fox translated by William Caxton from Middle Dutch bruun or bruyn, meaning brown (the color). [16][17] In themmid-19th century United States, the brown bear was termed "Old Ephraim" and sometimes as "Moccasin Joe". The brown bear(User arctos) is native to parts of northern Eurasia and North America. Its conservation status is currently "Least Concern." There are many subspecies within brown bear species, including the Atlas bear and the Himalayan brown bear.
